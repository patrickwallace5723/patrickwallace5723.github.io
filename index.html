Why AshGen

Coal fly ash is one of the largest industrial waste streams in the United States. American power plants still produce tens of millions of tons of fly ash every year — and more than a billion tons already sit in landfills and waste ponds across the country.

That waste doesn't just take up space. Fly ash contains heavy metals that can leach into surrounding soil and water sources, creating serious environmental risks for nearby communities. As a result, the EPA requires utilities to properly dispose of both newly generated and legacy fly ash — turning it into a costly and growing liability on their balance sheets.

AshGen will change that equation. By partnering with utilities as a fly ash offtaker, AshGen will help them reduce their environmental liability while transforming this underutilized waste stream into valuable, low-carbon products — including critical metals that support domestic advanced industries and construction materials that serve the modern built environment.
