Why AshGen

Coal fly ash is one of the largest industrial waste streams in the United States. American power plants still produce tens of millions of tons of coal ash every year and more than a billion tons already sit in open landfills and waste ponds across the country

That waste doesn't just take up space. Coal ash contains heavy metals that can leach into surrounding soil and water sources, creating serious environmental risks for nearby communities. As a result, the EPA requires utilities to properly dispose of both newly generated and legacy ash, turning it into a costly and growing liability

AshGen will change that equation. Our proprietary, zero-waste process, transforms this environmental liability into valuable, low-carbon products, including critical minerals for U.S. advanced industries and construction materials for the modern environment
